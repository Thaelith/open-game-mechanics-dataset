# Taxonomy

Categories are stable, lowercase identifiers used in mechanic IDs and folder names. Platforming is included as a specialized movement category because many games treat precision jump and wall interactions as their own design family.

## movement

A character action that changes how the player moves through space.

## combat

An interaction that creates threat, defense, damage, timing, or tactical pressure during conflict.

## camera

A viewpoint behavior that frames action and communicates spatial information to the player.

## physics

A world-system mechanic driven by forces, constraints, collision response, or simulated motion.

## platforming

A precision movement mechanic used to negotiate platforms, walls, gaps, and hazards.

## traversal

A locomotion tool that helps the player cross space, reach anchors, or move through level structures.

## puzzle

A rule-based challenge that asks the player to observe, reason, manipulate state, or sequence actions.

## stealth

A concealment or detection mechanic that lets players manage information, sound, sight, and risk.

## survival

A pressure mechanic that asks the player to manage bodily, environmental, or resource constraints over time.

## crafting

A production mechanic that turns resources, recipes, or stations into usable items or upgrades.

## economy

A value-exchange mechanic that governs currency, prices, scarcity, rewards, and transactions.

## progression

A long-term growth mechanic that changes player capability, goals, or account state.

## ai

A non-player behavior mechanic that controls sensing, intention, movement, and response.

## multiplayer

A social or networked mechanic that coordinates multiple players within shared rules.

## narrative

A story-state mechanic that presents, records, branches, or reacts to player decisions.

## ui_ux

An interface mechanic that presents game state, accepts decisions, or reduces player friction.

## accessibility

A player-support mechanic that makes controls, perception, difficulty, or information more adaptable.

## meta

A profile-level mechanic that operates across sessions, runs, or modes rather than only inside one encounter.

## procedural_generation

A content-generation mechanic that creates spaces, events, rewards, or structures from rules and seeds.

## vehicles

A controllable transport mechanic with dedicated movement, physics, resources, or damage behavior.

## rhythm

A timing mechanic where player input, scoring, or simulation aligns with musical or beat-based windows.

## time

A temporal mechanic that changes speed, ordering, deadlines, cooldowns, or history.

## horror

A fear-building mechanic that shapes vulnerability, uncertainty, attention, and tension.

## roguelike

A run-structure mechanic that supports replayability through randomness, reset, risk, or synergy.

## strategy

A planning mechanic that governs territory, information, unit roles, resources, or long-horizon decisions.

## simulation

A systemic mechanic where entities update according to rules and create emergent state over time.
